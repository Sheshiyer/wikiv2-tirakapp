# Messaging & Direction Summary (MDS)
## Tirak Dream Journeys - Source of Truth for Campaign Messaging

**Purpose:** This document serves as the master reference for all copywriting, advertising, email campaigns, social content, and PR materials. Every piece of marketing must align with the messaging framework defined here.

**Priority:** 80% Companion (Supply-Side) / 20% Traveler (Demand-Side)  
**Philosophy:** Supply Creates Demand - Build companion community first, travelers follow

---

## 1. Product Pitch (Hero Description)

### 30-Second Elevator Pitch
Tirak is a travel companion platform that connects travelers with verified Thai locals who share authentic cultural experiences—not scripted tours, but genuine hangouts. Companions set their own rates, keep 80-85% of earnings (vs. 30-40% industry standard), and showcase their unique personalities. Travelers discover hidden gems through locals who actually vibe with their interests. It's like Airbnb for human connection, not just accommodations.

### One-Liner
"Not a tour. A vibe."

### Tagline Options
- **Primary:** "Bangkok through the eyes of someone who actually lives here."
- **Supply-Side:** "Your city, your way, your earnings."
- **Demand-Side:** "Real people. Real Bangkok. Real connections."
- **Brand Mantra:** "Travel companions, not tour guides."

### 10-Second Hook (Social Media)
"Tired of 30% commissions? Tirak lets you keep 80-85% of your earnings showing travelers the real Bangkok. Same-day approval. No DSLR photoshoot required. Start today."

### 60-Second Deep Pitch (Investor/Press)
"Tirak is a two-sided marketplace solving a $12B problem: authentic local connection. On one side, Thai locals want to monetize their cultural knowledge but existing platforms (Withlocals, ToursByLocals) take exploitative 30-40% commissions and require months-long approval processes. On the other side, travelers—especially digital nomads—crave authentic experiences but face choice overload on Reddit or safety risks with unverified meetups. Tirak's solution: 15-20% commission (vs. 30-40%), same-day onboarding (vs. 2-6 months), and vibe-based matching. We're companion-first: 80% of marketing spend goes to supply acquisition because supply creates demand. Bangkok launch, Southeast Asia expansion, targeting 1,000 companions and 10,000 bookings in year one."

## 3. Use Cases (Specific Scenes and Contexts)

### Companion Use Cases (Supply-Side)

**Use Case #1: The Weekend Warrior**
- **Who:** Nok, 27, hotel front desk manager Mon-Fri, wants side income on weekends
- **Scenario:** Friday 6pm, Nok toggles availability "ON" for Saturday evening. Gets booking request at 8pm from Connor for Saturday 7pm bar crawl. Accepts, confirms meeting point (BTS Thong Lo), earns ฿1,700 for 4 hours.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Full-time job? No problem. Work weekends only, toggle off during the week. Earn ฿8,000-12,000/month hosting 4-5 Saturdays."

**Use Case #2: The Full-Time Hustler**
- **Who:** Pim, 29, quit corporate job to host full-time on Tirak
- **Scenario:** Hosts 5 experiences/week (3 evening, 2 full-day), earns ฿40,000-50,000/month. Built reputation to 4.9 stars with 200+ reviews. Premium travelers specifically request her.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Tirak isn't just side income—it's a career. Top hosts earn ฿40K-60K/month. Build your brand, set your rates, own your business."

**Use Case #3: The Spontaneous Earner**
- **Who:** Som, 24, university student with unpredictable schedule
- **Scenario:** Tuesday afternoon, classes canceled. Som toggles "available today" at 2pm. Gets booking request at 2:30pm for 4pm coffee hangout. Meets traveler at Cafe Ari, earns ฿800 for 2 hours.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Got free time today? Toggle 'available now' and earn money in 2 hours. Perfect for students, freelancers, anyone with flexible schedules."

**Use Case #4: The Niche Expert**
- **Who:** Kai, 32, tattoo artist who hosts "Bangkok underground art scene" experiences
- **Scenario:** Specializes in showing travelers street art, indie galleries, underground music venues. Charges premium (฿2,500 for 5 hours) because expertise is rare. Books 2-3x/week.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Not into temples or food tours? Create experiences around YOUR expertise—art, music, fitness, wellness, LGBTQ nightlife. Own your niche."

**Use Case #5: The Retiree Re-Engagement**
- **Who:** Somchai, 58, retired teacher, loves meeting foreigners and practicing English
- **Scenario:** Hosts cultural/historical experiences 2-3x/week (museums, temples, historical sites). Earns ฿15,000/month supplement to pension. Feels purpose and connection.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Retired but not ready to stop? Share your wisdom. Meet fascinating people. Earn extra income. Stay active and engaged."

### Traveler Use Cases (Demand-Side)

**Use Case #1: The Solo Digital Nomad**
- **Who:** Connor, 31, software engineer, solo in Bangkok for 3 months, lonely
- **Scenario:** Works 9am-5pm, free evenings/weekends. Books Nok for Saturday night bar crawl. They vibe, exchange Instagram, become friends. Connor re-books Nok 3 more times during stay.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Traveling solo? Cure loneliness. Meet locals who become real friends. Not just tour guides—actual human connections."

**Use Case #2: The First-Time Visitor (Orientation Mode)**
- **Who:** Sarah, 28, first time in Bangkok, overwhelmed by size and chaos
- **Scenario:** Lands Friday, books Sunday morning "Bangkok 101" with companion Pim. Learns BTS system, where to eat, safety tips, neighborhood recommendations. Feels confident rest of trip.
- **Messaging Hook:** "First time in Bangkok? Get oriented with a local. Learn the city in 3 hours. Avoid rookie mistakes. Start your trip right."

**Use Case #3: The Repeat Visitor (Hidden Gems Mode)**
- **Who:** Marcus, 34, visited Bangkok 4 times, wants to go deeper beyond tourist spots
- **Scenario:** Books Kai's "underground art scene" experience. Sees street art, indie galleries, meets local artists. Experiences Bangkok tourists never see.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Been to Bangkok 3 times? Go deeper. Find experiences tourists don't know exist. Unlock insider access."

**Use Case #4: The Couple's Date Night**
- **Who:** Alex & Jamie, 30s, couple traveling together, want romantic local experience
- **Scenario:** Book evening "rooftop bar + night market" experience with Som. She takes them to locals-only rooftop (no tourists), then hidden night market. Romantic and authentic.
- **Messaging Hook:** "Couples: skip the tourist trap sunset cruises. Get romantic local date night vibes. Hidden rooftops, secret spots, authentic moments."

**Use Case #5: The Corporate Team Retreat**
- **Who:** Tech startup, 8 employees, team retreat in Bangkok
- **Scenario:** Book group experience "Bangkok food adventure" with 2 companions. Team bonding through authentic local food exploration (not generic catering).
- **Messaging Hook:** "Corporate teams: ditch the boring hotel conference room. Bond over authentic local experiences. We handle group bookings (up to 10 people)."

## 4. Target Audiences (Demographics & Psychographics)

### Primary Audience: Thai Companions (80% Marketing Focus)

**Core Demographics:**
- **Age:** 22-35 years old (sweet spot: 25-30)
- **Gender:** 60% female, 40% male (platform welcomes all genders)
- **Location:** Bangkok (Sukhumvit, Thonglor, Ari, Silom), Chiang Mai (Old City, Nimman), Phuket (Patong, Old Town), Pattaya
- **Income:** ฿15,000-35,000/month from day job, seeking ฿8,000-15,000/month side income
- **Education:** Bachelor's degree or working professionals (hospitality, tourism, teaching, service industry)
- **English Level:** Conversational to fluent (IELTS 5.5-7.0 equivalent)
- **Occupation:** Hotel staff, teachers, bartenders, tour guides (tired of 30% commissions), university students, freelancers, retirees

**Psychographics:**
- **Personality:** Social, outgoing, entrepreneurial, proud of Thai culture
- **Values:** Autonomy, fairness, authenticity, community, financial independence
- **Frustrations:** Exploited by platforms (30-40% commissions), rigid onboarding (months wait), no control over pricing/schedule, safety concerns with unvetted travelers
- **Goals:** Earn sustainable side income, meet interesting people, share authentic Thai culture, work on own schedule, build reputation that compounds
- **Online Behavior:** Active on Facebook (Thai groups), Instagram (@bangkokfoodie, @explorechile), LINE (Thai messaging app), TikTok
- **Influencers/Media:** BK Magazine, Bangkok Post Lifestyle, TimeOut Bangkok, Thai lifestyle influencers

**Segmentation (Sub-Personas):**
1. **The Weekend Warrior** (40% of companions) - Has full-time job, hosts weekends only
2. **The Full-Time Hustler** (15%) - Quit day job, hosts 15-20 experiences/month, earning ฿40K+
3. **The Student Side-Gig** (25%) - University student, flexible schedule, earns ฿5K-10K/month
4. **The Niche Expert** (10%) - Specializes in specific interests (art, music, wellness, LGBTQ scene)
5. **The Retiree Re-Engagement** (10%) - 50-65 years old, retired, seeks purpose and supplemental income

### Secondary Audience: Travelers (20% Marketing Focus)

**Core Demographics:**
- **Age:** 28-38 years old (sweet spot: 30-35)
- **Gender:** 55% male, 45% female
- **Location:** US (San Francisco, Austin, NYC, Seattle), EU (Berlin, London, Amsterdam, Barcelona), Australia (Melbourne, Sydney)
- **Income:** $75,000-120,000/year (can afford $50-100/day experiences)
- **Education:** Bachelor's or Master's degree
- **Occupation:** Digital nomads, remote workers (software engineers, designers, consultants, content creators, marketers)
- **Travel Status:** Solo travelers, 2-6 month stays per city, location-independent

**Psychographics:**
- **Personality:** Curious, open-minded, socially comfortable, values authenticity over luxury
- **Values:** Authenticity, connection, efficiency, safety/trust, flexibility, Instagram-worthy experiences
- **Frustrations:** Group tours feel scripted, Reddit recommendations overwhelming, random bar encounters unreliable, existing guide directories sketchy, hours wasted researching
- **Goals:** Experience "real" Bangkok (not tourist traps), make genuine local friends, efficient time use (work 9-5, explore evenings/weekends), Instagram content, cultural learning beyond Wikipedia
- **Online Behavior:** Reddit (r/digitalnomad, r/Thailand, r/solotravel), Nomad List forums, Instagram (travel influencers), travel blogs (Nomadic Matt, Lost LeBlanc)
- **Influencers/Media:** Nomad List, Remote Year, Digital Nomad subreddit, travel YouTubers

**Segmentation (Sub-Personas):**
1. **The Solo Digital Nomad** (50% of travelers) - Working remotely, 2-6 months in Bangkok, seeks social connection
2. **The First-Time Visitor** (25%) - Overwhelmed by Bangkok, needs orientation and safety guidance
3. **The Repeat Visitor** (15%) - Been to Bangkok 3+ times, wants deeper hidden-gem experiences
4. **The Couple Traveler** (10%) - Romantic getaway, seeks authentic date night experiences
